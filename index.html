⚓ HORMUZ

Run the oil. Hold the strait.

New game

An asymmetric two-player game played across two devices. The United States runs a visible fleet of oil tankers through the Strait of Hormuz, trying to deliver more than the target amount of oil before the clock runs out — while managing its popularity. Iran is the hidden, weaker side: it secretly places mines, ambushes, and sensors, springing surprise attacks to sink tankers and run out the clock.

Host a game

Create a room, pick your side, and share the room code with your opponent. They play the other side.

Join a game

Enter the room code your opponent shared. You'll automatically play the side they didn't take.

How to play
  • Two devices, one per player. One person hosts and shares the room code; the other joins. Each side sees only its own information.
  • Iran setup: place hidden mines (nodes or lanes), ambushes, and sensors within budget, then End Setup.
  • US turn: click a tanker to select it, then click an adjacent node to move (limited steps/turn). Reach an exit to bank its oil. Play US ability cards. End Turn.
  • Iran turn: place any remaining assets, play Iran ability cards, End Turn.
  • US wins by delivering more than the target oil. Iran wins on the clock, by collapsing US popularity, or by sinking the whole fleet.
